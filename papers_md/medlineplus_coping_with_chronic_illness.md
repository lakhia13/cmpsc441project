Having a long-term, or chronic, illness can disrupt your life in many ways. You may often be tired and inpain. Your illness might affect your appearance or your physical abilities and independence. You may not be able to work, causing financial problems. For children, chronic illnesses can be frightening, because they may not understand why this is happening to them.

These changes can cause stress, anxiety, and anger. If they do, it is important to seek help. A trained counselor can help you develop strategies to regain a feeling of control. Support groups might help, too. You will find that you are not alone, and you may learn some new tips on how to cope.

You may be able to manage your illness better if you learn more about it. It is important toevaluate the informationthat you find, to make sure that it is reliable. It is also important tofind a health care providerthat you can trust.
